A Quick and Easy Tuna Recipe with Rice and Veggies

Home Page

Description

The recipe consists of a creamy and savory tuna on salty and sour rice with juicy fresh vegetable slices. This is a go-to recipe of mine when I need a reasonably balanced meal fast. The recipe was inspired by an onigiri recipe which I followed increasingly lazily, and in doing so, I developed an efficient, customizable process that yields a fairly balanced and flavorful meal in 10-15 minutes I always have the necessary ingredients because I designed the recipe to be flexible and made from pantry and fridge staples in my home. The flexibility is what makes this technique so reliable in my busy life, so I encourage you to experiment with the basic recipe to find new combinations that suit your preferences!

Main Ingredients

  • Chunk light/preferred tuna
  • Mayonnaise
  • Low-sodium/preferred soy sauce
  • White rice
  • Rice wine vinegar
  • Bell pepper/preferred fresh vegetable
  • Furikake strongly recommended

Steps

  1. Step 1. Start the Rice

    First, pour your rice into a pot, cover it with about an inch of water and a lid, and bring it to a boil for 8 minutes or until tender. For now, just watch to make sure it doesn't boil over. If you have a preferred method for cooking rice, feel free to use that instead.

  2. Step 2. Mix Your Creamy Tuna Topping

    Drain your can of tuna and add it to a bowl. Add a heaped tablespoon of mayonnaise and a splash of soy sauce to taste. Here is where you can really experiment with flavors, and I recommend that you do so while tasting along the way. I will usually also add a squirt of sriracha, some nutritional yeast, and a few drops of sesame oil to round out the flavor profile. I have also used mirin, teriyaki sauce, seaweed, and Japanese barbecue sauce. It all depends on how I'm feeling in the moment because the experimentation is all part of the fun. Mix the ingredients together with a fork while making sure to mash up the bigger tuna chunks as you go along.

  3. Step 3. Finish Your Rice

    If your rice isn't ready, you may want to get a head start on the next step first and return. Otherwise, strain as much water as you can through a gap in the lid or with a colander (yes, i know the internet told you not to strain rice, but i got much better and easier results after switching to the soggy strain method. sue me). Lightly salt your drained rice and stir with a splash of rice wine vinegar as the residual heat cooks off some of the remaining moisture.

  4. Step 4. Prep Your Vegetable

    No need to overcomplicate this step: simply cut up your bell pepper into whatever shape you like. You may try other veggies such as cucumber if you prefer. I personally find that a red bell pepper has the best flavor and nutritional qualities for this dish. Just make sure you put some kind of vegetable in your final dish, fatass. Porky. Hog. Double-wide.

  5. Step 5. Serve

    Finally, lay a bed of your seasoned rice in a bowl, spread your tuna over it, add your furikake if you have it, and top with your fresh vegetable. By using the rice cook time to prep your other ingredients, you can easily finish this balanced meal within 10 minutes.
